They're both chlorine, so why keep both around? Because they do opposite jobs. **Shock is a fast, high-dose treatment; tablets are slow-release daily maintenance.** Most well-run pools use both — tablets to hold a steady sanitizer level, shock to knock down problems tablets can't. Here's how to tell them apart and when to reach for each.

## What each one actually does

**Chlorine tablets** (usually trichlor pucks) sit in a floater, feeder, or skimmer and dissolve slowly, releasing a small, steady amount of chlorine over days. Their job is to maintain your baseline free-chlorine residual so the water stays sanitized between your bigger interventions.

**Pool shock** is a large, fast-dissolving dose — cal-hypo, liquid chlorine (sodium hypochlorite), or dichlor — designed to spike free chlorine quickly to **breakpoint**, oxidizing chloramines, killing algae, and clearing organic load. It's a treatment, not a maintenance drip.

## Side by side

| | Chlorine tablets | Pool shock |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Speed | Slow release over days | Fast, acts in hours |
| Purpose | Maintain daily sanitizer | Knock down algae, chloramines, contamination |
| Chlorine level | Small, steady | Large spike to breakpoint |
| Typical form | Trichlor pucks | Cal-hypo, liquid, dichlor |
| Adds stabilizer (CYA)? | Yes (trichlor) | Only dichlor does |
| Swim wait | Minimal if levels stay normal | [Wait until FC is 1–5 ppm](/blog/how-long-after-shocking-can-you-swim/) |

## The CYA catch worth understanding

Trichlor tablets are **stabilized** — every puck adds cyanuric acid (CYA). That's helpful up to a point (it protects chlorine from the sun), but a pool run on tablets alone slowly creeps up in CYA. Too much CYA and your chlorine stops working well, which is a leading cause of a [pool that stays green even after shocking](/blog/why-is-my-pool-still-green-after-shocking/).

This is the core reason to shock with an *unstabilized* product to balance things out:

- **[Cal-hypo](/cal-hypo/)** adds no CYA (it does add some calcium) — a strong choice for scheduled shocking.
- **[Liquid chlorine](/liquid-chlorine/)** adds neither CYA nor calcium — the cleanest option for frequent dosing or pools that already have high CYA.

## When to use which

**Reach for tablets when:** you want set-and-forget daily sanitizing and your CYA is in range. This is your everyday baseline.

**Reach for shock when:**
- Combined chlorine (that "chlorine smell") is climbing — [here's how often that happens](/blog/how-often-should-you-shock-your-pool/).
- The water is green, cloudy, or you had heavy use, a storm, or a contamination event.
- You're opening or closing the pool for the season.

## They work together

The typical rhythm: tablets hold your free chlorine day to day, and you shock every week or two — and after big events — to reset the water. Match the shock product to your chemistry (cal-hypo or liquid if CYA is already high from tablets), and dose it accurately with [the cal-hypo calculator](/cal-hypo/) or [liquid chlorine calculator](/liquid-chlorine/) so you hit breakpoint without overshooting.
